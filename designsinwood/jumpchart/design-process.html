Our Process

Of course, the essence of a room will always dictate the type of furniture needed but there are other factors, not always obvious, that should be taken in to consideration.

The way light enters the room and how it changes throughout the day, highlighting the importance of the colour palate.

How the furniture will appear from different aspects of the house; through a sitting room door, looking along a hallway or the view through a window as approached from the garden, terrace or driveway. And although built for the present how will the design work for the future.

When familiar with your surroundings these small observations may be overlooked but help to assist with the development of any room.

The room may still be in the planning stage. This is an ideal time to start exploring the furniture layout. It may lead to changes in door and window positioning also interior fixtures and fittings such as electric sockets, light switches or the way an entry door is hinged.

Each Plan is drawn by hand and although computer generated design is a popular way to deliver a room layout, working, developing and presenting ideas using pen and paper is an effective method as it helps to build a level of familiarity with a project.

It’s a myth you cannot introduce period furniture to a modern house or contemporary furniture to a period property. We know because we’ve done it.

It is for us to develop an understanding of individual’s expectations, draw ideas from the surroundings and transfer into the design.
